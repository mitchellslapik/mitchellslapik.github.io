<p>Hello! I'm an MD/PhD candidate at <a href="https://med.uth.edu/">McGovern Medical School</a>, originally from Austin. Psychiatric illness is one of the hardest problems in medicine, sitting at the intersection of genetics, neural circuits, lived experience, and society. I use machine learning and computational modeling to untangle these forces and bring greater precision to psychiatric diagnosis and treatment. More broadly, I believe this work illuminates longstanding questions about the mind, suffering, and meaning.</p>

<p>I am supported by an <a href="https://researchtraining.nih.gov/programs/fellowships/F30">NIH F30 Fellowship</a> and a <a href="https://www.uth.edu/ccts/education-training/tl1-program">TL1 Translational and Clinical Science Predoctoral Fellowship</a>. My dissertation, <em>Vision: More than Meets the Eye</em>, examines parallels between biological and artificial intelligence in visual processing. My work has been published in <em>Neural Computation</em>, <em>Journal of Vision</em>, and <em>Cerebellum</em>, with an additional manuscript under review at <em>Nature Neuroscience</em>. Before my doctoral training, I studied philosophy in the Honors Program at <a href="https://www.swarthmore.edu/">Swarthmore College</a> and worked as a research assistant at <a href="https://www.jhu.edu/">Johns Hopkins University</a>. Alongside my research, I have worked as a crisis counselor and shadowed extensively in inpatient psychiatry. I am currently applying to research-track psychiatry residency programs.</p>
