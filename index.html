Mitchell Slapik

I am currently an MD/PhD student in the Medical Scientist Training Program at McGovern Medical School. I study computational neuroscience in Valentin Dragoi's lab, where I focus on how neural circuits process visual information. Previously, I did research on the cerebellum in Cherie Marvel's lab at Johns Hopkins University, and studied philosophy at Swarthmore College and University of Oxford. Ultimately, I plan to specialize in psychiatry and use machine learning to better understand and treat psychiatric disorders. Outside of work, I volunteer at Crisis Text Line and play jazz saxophone in The Chirp Chirps and Bayou City Funk.

For more information, see my CV or Google Scholar.

Made this visual of Larry Abbott's work on fly navigation. Flies represent flight direction with a bump of neural activity in a certain brain area, and wind direction with another bump. In visual #neuroscience, stimuli can be presented very rapidly. However, neurons have a conduction delay before they respond. So how do we know which spikes go with which stimuli? Reverse correlations can simultaneously determine the preferred stimulus and the conduction delay. pic.twitter.com/GHWj4ocL4B
